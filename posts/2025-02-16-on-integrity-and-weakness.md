---
title: On Integrity and Weakness
---

I have always considered myself a person of integrity. But it turns out I'm
weaker than I thought.

<span></span><!--more-->

The basic values upon which I built my humanity are honesty, courage and honor.
Much of this is inspired by the tales of old and the idea of the loyal knight.
Until recently, I lived up to these values. I was always transparent and brave
enough to do what I thought was right. I had the courage to make the right
decisions, no matter how hard the situation.

But then I betrayed someone I loved. I did the most despicable and vile thing a
human being can do to a loved one. At the time, I was unhappy. Unhappy with my
life, unhappy with my family, unhappy with my job and unhappy with my romantic
relationship. But still, I realized what I joke I was, and that I lacked
everything that I stood for.

I lacked the honesty to express my true feelings.

I lacked the courage to act when it was called for, and to avoid inflicting the
pain I caused. 

I lacked the honor to stand by my values and do the right thing.

I did a terrible thing, and I will remember and regret it forever. But I have
to move on. I need to learn and rebuild my spirit with the newly obtained
knowledge I have of myself. I must do these things, because the other option
would be to put a bullet through my head. And I'm done with that kind of
weakness. I cannot repair what I've destroyed, but I can make sure that from
now on, I'll only build, and never destroy. I can make sure to bring happiness,
and never pain.

Today, I feel free of the burden of living up to someone I'm not. And that's
freed me to become a better version of myself. Today, I understand that I am
what I always thought I was, and that I am not. I understand that I've never
loved, for if I had loved, I would not have acted as I did.

But I feel at peace. At peace knowing the person I really am. At peace knowing
that I can do good. At peace knowing that I need not hurt others. I will not
forgive who I was, for such a thing should not be forgiven. I will despise him
and hate him until the moment I draw my last breath. But I will also love him,
because he returned my integrity. Because he made me understand who I am today,
and who I'll be tomorrow.

I am writing this, because I believe in honesty, and courage, and honor.
I am writing this, because I deserve whatever backlash comes.
I am writing this, because I don't want it trapped in my heart.

If you are a friend, I hope you will forgive me. As for everyone else, feel free
to do as you please, for I doubt there is worse punishment than the one I
submit myself to myself.
